At first glance, this topic might seem quite grand, but it actually concerns every single programmer.

When developers switch jobs, it is highly likely that they will bring the proprietary technology and code from their former employers with them, thereby saving the new company the research and development costs and time. To the new employer, this is undoubtedly the most valuable asset a new hire can bring. As long as the two companies are not direct competitors, most people nowadays won't look too closely or pursue any action. But in all honesty, is this really just the fault of the social environment?

I remember many years ago, I wrote a post about selling game bot programs to make money. In plain terms: when faced with money, what exactly are you willing to sell out? Everything?

Our ethical baselines are being crossed over and over again—and this isn't just about programmers. This issue plagues the entire industry, from a single individual or a small team, all the way up to a company or society as a whole. Once again, the baseline has been broken.
